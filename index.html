Hertha Ayrton

bio

Phoebe Sarah Hertha Ayrton was born on April 28, 1854 in the U.K.
The third of eight children with no father, she had to hold a lot of responsibility from an early age.
She was introduced to science and math by the age of nine.
Hertha went to Girton College in Cambridge. While she was there she constructed a sphygmomanometer (blood pressure meter).
Hertha was awarded her a Bachelor of Science degree in 1881.
Hertha married William Edward Ayrton in 1885. In 1886 her daughter Barbara Bodichon Ayrton was born.
Hertha died at age 65 on 26 August 1923 in Sussex, England. For more info about her accomplishments and work see below.

more about me

I was an engineer, mathmatician, physicist and inventor, and suffragette. I was awarded the Hughes Medal by the Royal Society for her work on electric arcs and ripples in sand and water.
I won many scientific awards, taught in highschools, had published works, and worked for social activisim.
